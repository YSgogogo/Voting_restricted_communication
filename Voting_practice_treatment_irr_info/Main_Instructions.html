Structure of the experiment

  • You will face 15 rounds in today's experiment.
  • Each round is independent of other rounds.
  • Each round is equally likely to be selected for your earnings.
  • You might be matched with different participants as group members in each round.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each round seriously.

Structure of the round

Stage 1: Information

There is a state called ''unknown state''. This unknown state is either ''RED'' or ''BLUE''.
For each unknown state there are two boxes: Box A and Box B.
The composition of the boxes and their relation to the “unknown state” is as follows.
    • Box A has 8 (eight) balls in total and Box B has 7 (seven) balls in total.
    • If the unknown state is ''RED'' then Box A has 7 (seven) red balls and 1 (one) blue ball, while Box B has 4 (four) red balls and 3 (three) blue balls.
    • If the unknown state is ''BLUE'' then Box A has 7 (seven) blue balls and 1 (one) red ball, while Box B has 4 (four) blue balls and 3 (three) red balls.



  • At the beginning of each round, every 5 participants in this lab will be randomly selected into one group, and each participant will be given a unique ID for that round.
  • In your group, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) to determine the “unknown state” - either ''RED'' or ''BLUE''. No one will be told which state is actually selected!
  • For each of you, the computer will flip a coin (50-50) again to determine a box given the already selected unknown state - either Box A or Box B.
  • For each of you, the computer will randomly and independently pick a ball from your selected box - either or .
  • For each of you, the computer will randomly and independently assign you a triangle - either or .
  • The triangle that each group member got will be shown to other group members, BUT the selected box and selected ball will not be shown to other group members. Your box and ball information is private.
  • After knowing this information, you will be asked to choose whom you want to send your private information (i.e., your ball & your box) to:
    • a)   send to a player who got
    b)   send to a player who got
    c)   do not send to anyone

    N.B. 1. If there is more than one group member (except you) who got the same triangle, the computer will randomly send your private information to one of them; 2. if no group member (except you) got the purple triangle, option a) will not be available; if no group member (except you) got the orange triangle, option b) will not be available.

Stage 2: Voting

  • Once all group members have finished Stage 1, you will be shown the following:
    • 1)   the left table will have more box information if other group members choose to send their private box information to you.
    2)   the network diagram in the middle includes the flow of information.
    3)   you need to vote for the ''unknown” state that was chosen at the beginning: RED or BLUE.
